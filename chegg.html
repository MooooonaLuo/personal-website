OVERVIEW

The past summer, I interned as a UX designer at Chegg's Engagement UX team. I took ownership of the two projects I worked on. In my capstone project, I worked in a cross-functional team to improve the discoverability and engagement of Chegg's Course Dashboard. Although I'm not able to share any design details at this moment due to the NDA, I'll talk about the design process, challenges, and what I've learned.

MY CONTRIBUTIONS

  • Led the design of two new features to increase the discoverability and engagement on Chegg's Course Dashboard.
  • Collaborated with the UX researcher to conduct user research and iterated design based on the findings.
  • Proactively presented my work in progress to collect feedback from stakeholders, designers, and accessibility experts.
  • Built the content design system with the content design team.

CHALLENGES I OVERCAME

My team was double the size of a normal team, and communication in such a big team was challenging.

I worked cross-functionally with four interns, each has a manager. The interns took ownership of the project, but I still needed to get feedback from the managers. Besides, there were many stakeholders who are less involved but needed to be kept in the loop. I was clueless about how to handle this situation at first, but over time, I developed the skill to communicate with the right person at the right cadence. I also employed different ways to share updates on my design and collect feedback. For example, when I had difficulty scheduling a meeting with our managers, I shared a 5-minute video walkthrough of my design and posted recaps on Slack. This chart shows how I communicated with different skateholders.



Another challenge was to collect and synthesize feedback from people not directly involved in my project.

I proactively seek feedback from designers in the broader teams' meetings and people in other roles. I found it tricky at the beginning because first, I didn't have enough time to cover everything, and second, people had different levels of knowledge about my project. How did I efficiently collect feedback from a large group of people? I started by introducing the context and emphasizing what feedback I was looking for specifically; instead of covering everything I designed, I focused on one or two parts that mattered the most.

As I got more feedback, some appeared to contradict each other. I realized that declining a piece of feedback was indeed harder than taking one. To better handle feedback, I kept the high-level project goal in mind and respectfully declined feedback that was out of scope or less important to the goal.

WHAT I'VE LEARNED

Hard skills:

  • Using Confluence for design documentation and team communication.
  • Creating prototypes with a design system.
  • Learning accessibility design systematically and applying the knowledge to design.

Soft skills:

  • Proactively communicating with stakeholders when there's a blocker to get necessary inputs to move forward.
  • Constantly collecting feedback from my partners and the broader UX team. Being specific about what feedback I'm looking for.
  • Making decisions that fulfill both user goals and business goals. Understanding how my work contributes to higher-level goals of the company.
  • Closely collaborating with UX researchers, content designers, and product managers.
  • Handling dependency across different projects through effective communication.
